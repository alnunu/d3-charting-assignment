Correlations Discovered Between Poverty and Smoking

This chart displays the percentage of the population that smokes as well as the percentage considered in poverty for each state within the US. As a general trend, the smoking population percentage does correlate with the percentage of people in poverty. This may be due to a variety of reasons, some theories are based on education being correlated with income. More educated populations tend to smoke less because they are aware of the effects. Other theories center around stress management. Individuals in poverty tend to have more chronic stress, making them more likely to resort recreational drug use for relief.
